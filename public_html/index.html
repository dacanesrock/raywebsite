About our Candidate:

Ray McKinnon

Ray McKinnon has been married to Kelly McKinnon since 2005. Together they have four boys (Quinton, Alex, Malik, and Mikel), a dog, and a cat. Ray was raised in Greensboro, NC where he graduated from Dudley High School. He is also a graduate of John Wesley University in High Point, NC.Kelly and Ray are also former foster parents who proudly adopted three of their children from the foster care system here in Mecklenburg County. Kelly and Ray’s sons are all students at Charlotte Mecklenburg Schools at every level.

Ray is currently the pastor at a local United Methodist Church in Charlotte, NC. His church works closely with the Brookhill Community advocating for safe and affordable housing for the more than three hundred folks who call Brookhill home. In service to the community, Ray is also a commissioner of the Charlotte Housing Authority’s Board of Commissioners. Micah 6:8 is a guiding scripture verse for his life following the three principles outlined therein: Do justice, love mercy, and walk humbly.

Together with two of his friends, Lula Dualeh and Sebastian Feculak, he founded New South Progressives to advocate, on the local level, for justice, equity, and inclusion at every level.Because Ray believes that every person deserves the ability to move grow and prosper, he serves as a member of the Leading on Opportunity Council. He is also a member of the Southend Board Committee (on account of his work at his church).

Ray wants to be your next County Commissioner so that we will do justice, love mercy, and walk humbly.

Top Issues

Our Mission

Affordable Housing
  • Work with City & County to leverage our resources both financial and human to develop and implement plans to create more affordable housing
  • Work with Private and Faith organizations to leverage existing resources (land & Buildings) to create additional affordable Housing
  • Work with existing Apartments owners to make available vouchers and services to support potential renters into new apartment complexes
Public Education
  • Engage with the School board to leverage financial resources to provide for all schools.
  • Develop partnerships across our schools to help improve reading, writing and math at all levels.
  • Support the need for pre-k for all 4-year old’s in the County
Opportunity Gap
  • Increase the living wage to $15.00 an hour for County employees.
  • Encourage the other municipalities to follow (Charlotte has already done this) and private sector to follow our lead.
  • Provide services or programs for adult financial education, home ownership programs, and education for all children.
About our Vision

Our Democracy Vision

Politics and Vision: Continuity and Innovation in Western Political Thought is a work of political theory by Princeton Emeritus Professor Sheldon S. Wolin. Part One, consisting of ten chapters and first published in 1960, distinguishes political philosophy from philosophy in general and traces political philosophy from its Platonic origins to modern day Part Two, consisting of seven chapters and published (along with Part One) in a 2004 expanded edition, traces the development of political thought from Marx, Nietzsche, and others up to the late 20th century.
